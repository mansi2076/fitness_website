Baked Sweet Potato Fries

Crisp and Satisfying Side Dish


The Dish

These baked sweet potato fries are a satisfying side dish for any lunch or dinner. Sweet potatoes are an excellent source of fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making them a much more wholesome substitute for traditional white potatoes. To get these baked fries crisp, we use a little cornstarch coating. The cornstarch forms a protective barrier around the potato, crisping the outside to a nice golden brown. Spreading the fries as far apart as possible on the baking sheet also enhances crispness, allowing for more airflow. If you’re cooking for a group — doubling or tripling this recipe — we recommend using a couple of baking sheets and rotating them halfway through the baking process.

Hacking this Recipe

You can prep the sweet potatoes well in advance and freeze the fresh-cut sweet potatoes until you’re ready to season and bake. Frozen sweet potato fries will need a little longer baking time, about 8-10 additional minutes.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ oz. sweet potatoes(cut into sticks)
  • ¼ tsp. cornstarch
  • ¼ tsp. garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp. onion powder
  • ¼ tsp. paprika
  • 1 tsp. olive oil

Preperation Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F or 220°C. Line a medium baking sheet with aluminum foil and lightly grease with pan release spray.
  2. Place sweet potato fries in a medium bowl. Add cornstarch, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and olive oil; stir or toss until the sweet potatoes are thoroughly coated.
  3. Transfer the seasoned potatoes to the prepared baking sheet. Spread out in an even layer, making sure each sweet potato fry has plenty of space to cook and crisp.
  4. Bake for 20 minutes or until golden brown and crispy, flipping the fries halfway through. Remove from the oven and season to taste with salt and pepper.
