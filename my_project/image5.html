Parmesan Garlic Bread

With Whole Wheat Bread and Olive Oil


The Dish

Enhance your Italian-themed main dish with a nice, wholesome side of parmesan garlic bread. Our version of garlic bread is made using whole wheat bread, instead of white bread or white rolls, and olive oil instead of butter. These simple substitutions do not degrade the flavor or appeal of this popular side. Every member of the family will be requesting this garlic bread for any pasta dinner, dunkable soup, or sop-worthy stew.

Time Saving Tips

For the whole wheat bread, we calculated each slice to be approximately 2oz (35g), averaging 2 slices of bread per person for this recipe.

Ingredients

  • ½ tsp. olive oil
  • ½ tsp. garlic (peeled and minced)
  • 2 ½ oz. whole wheat bread (2 slices)
  • 2 tsp. parmesan (grated)

Preperation Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F or 200°C and line a small baking sheet with aluminum foil.
  2. In a small bowl combine olive oil and minced garlic, stirring to combine. Place the whole wheat bread slices on the prepared baking sheet and drizzle with the garlic oil.
  3. Sprinkle the bread with grated parmesan and place in the oven. Bake for 4-5 minutes or until the bread is toasted and lightly golden brown.
  4. Remove from the oven and serve warm.
