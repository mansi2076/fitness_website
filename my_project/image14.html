Rustic Red Rice

With Onion, Peppers, and Tomatoes


The Dish

This rustic red rice recipe pulls inspiration from the famed Arroz Rojo, a Mexican-style rice cooked with fragrant and flavorful ingredients: garlic, onion, peppers, and tomatoes. To boost our overall nutritional value, we opted for brown rice, adding a little more protein and fiber to this side dish. Brown rice is a fabulous whole grain. Being “whole” means that brown rice contains all parts of the grain: the fibrous bran, the protein-rich germ, and the hearty-carb endosperm. White rice, on the other hand, has had the bran and germ removed. Try this fantastic side dish with either meat or veggie main dishes. It’s a perfect accompaniment to tacos, burritos, or Mexican cuisine-inspired recipes too!

Time Saving Tips

The whole grain nature of brown rice means that it takes a little while longer to cook than most other rice options. To save on time, cook this side dish ahead of time, then cool, refrigerate, and reheat (either on the stovetop with a little extra broth or in the microwave) before serving.

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp. olive oil
  • ¼ cup brown rice
  • 1 tsp. garlic (peeled and minced)
  • 2 Tbsp. red onion (peeled and minced)
  • 2 Tbsp. green bell pepper (diced)
  • ¼ cup canned crushed tomatoes
  • ¾ cup low sodium vegetable broth

Preperation Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, add the garlic, onion, and bell peppers, stir to combine, and sauté until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add the rice, stir to combine with the veggies, and lightly toast for one minute more.
  4. the crushed tomatoes and broth, stir to combine, and bring to a simmer.
  5. Once simmering, reduce heat to low, cover, and continue to cook for 30-40 minutes or until the rice is tender.
  6. Remove from the heat, tent the lid, and set aside to rest for 5 minutes before serving. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
