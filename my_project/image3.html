Stuffed Pepper Soup

With Ground Turkey and Bell Peppers


The Dish

Just like the classic stuffed peppers dish, this soup is made using easy-to-source ingredients like ground turkey, green bell peppers, red bell peppers, tomatoes, and rice. Red bell peppers bring a level of natural sweetness while green bell peppers add verdant, earthy pops. Ground turkey replaces the traditional ground beef for a leaner protein option. Brown rice is a fabulous whole grain. Being a whole grain means that brown rice contains all parts of the grain: the fibrous bran, the protein-rich germ, and the hearty-carb endosperm. White rice, on the other hand, has had the bran and germ removed. Since these are the most nutritious parts of the grain, brown rice is our go-to for maximum nutrition.

Time Saving Tips

We also use pre-cooked brown rice in this recipe. We’re notorious for making or cooking batches of basic pantry staples that will be frequently used throughout the week — quinoa, brown rice, etc. This really cuts down on the overall cooking time as well as day-of prep. For our fans of prepping ahead, this soup is a fabulous option. Cook, chill, refrigerate, and reheat when ready to enjoy.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup green bell pepper(diced)
  • ½ cup red bell pepper(diced)
  • 2 Tbsp. red onion(peeled and diced)
  • 1 tsp. garlic(peeled and minced)
  • 4 oz. ground turkey
  • 1 cup canned diced tomatoes
  • ½ cup low sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 cup brown rice(cooked)
  • 1 ½ Tbsp. scallions(chopped)

Preperation Instructions

  1. Moderately spritz a medium saucepan with pan release spray and heat oven to medium.
  2. Add the green bell peppers, red bell peppers, onion, and garlic to the saucepan and sauté for 2-3 minutes, or until tender-crisp and fragrant.
  3. Crumble the ground turkey into the saucepan, stir to combine with the veggies, and continue to cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the ground turkey has browned and is cooked through.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes and broth, stir to combine with the turkey and veggies, and bring to a simmer. Simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the cooked rice to the saucepan, stir to combine the soup, and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  6. Spoon or ladle soup into a bowl and garnish with chopped scallions before serving.
