Homemade Sausage Breakfast Platter

With a Sunny Side Up Egg


The Dish

This recipe is partially inspired by the popular English-style breakfast, with just a little nutritional modification and simplifying for a quick and easy preparation, whether prepared during busy weekdays or weekends. Homemade turkey sausage patties — well-seasoned with sage, thyme, and paprika — are skillet-seared and served with a sunny side up egg. A pan-roasted tomato and toasted whole wheat bread round out this morning meal. If you’re still feeling hungry, feel free to serve this hearty meal with a side dish of fruit salad or a warm bowl of grain porridge.

Time Saving Tips

This turkey sausage recipe can easily be made a day or two in advance, either the raw, seasoned mixture or skillet cooked and cooled. You can also freeze the cooked sausage, thaw, and reheat when you’re ready to eat.

Ingredients

  • 3 oz. lean ground turkey
  • ¼ tsp. garlic powder
  • ¼ tsp. dried sage
  • ¼ tsp. dried thyme
  • ¼ tsp. paprika
  • 2 oz. egg(1 each)
  • 2 oz. tomato(small, halved)
  • 1 ½ oz. whole wheat bread (1 slice, toasted)

Preperation Instructions

  1. Lightly grease a medium non-stick skillet with pan release spray and slowly heat over medium-high.
  2. Place ground turkey in a medium bowl and season with garlic powder, sage, thyme, paprika, and a small pinch of salt. Using your hands, mix to combine and divide into 2 patties.
  3. Once the skillet is hot, add the turkey sausage patties and cook for about 2 minutes per side, or until cooked through. Remove the sausage patties from the skillet, set aside on a serving plate, and lightly grease the skillet with additional cooking spray.
  4. Add the egg and halved tomato, cut side down, to the skillet; reduce heat to medium-low and cover. Cook for 2-3 minutes or until the egg is cooked to your liking and the tomato is tender.
  5. Lightly season both with salt and pepper and transfer to the plate with the sausage.
  6. Lastly, cut whole wheat toast into wedges and add to the plate before serving.
